We are a non-profit organisation

that helps people in the US, the UK and Ireland cope with their mental health issues through the care and love of their dogs

Free of charge
 

We have a team of dedicated professional dog trainers

who have volunteered to help people train their paw partners and lead a better social and profesional life

The assisted training we offer will enable you to train your own psychiatric service dog.

This type of dog takes about 120 hours to train and does not require as many hours as a guide dog or a hearing dog. The psychiatric service dog is trained to respond to the mental disability of a person in a uniquely suited way.

These types of service dogs are shaped by their bond with their owner/handlers. PSDs are trained to assist with opening and closing tasks, keep their users focused, provide emotional support, keep you alert, bring medication to you, ground you and interrupt repetitive behaviour.

Your paw partner can be a pillar for your mental health and help improve your social life.

Our association can guide you through the training process and enable you to confidently navigate the public space.

We can help you cope with issues like anxiety, depression, PTSD, panic disorder, obsessive-compulsive disorder and autism.

Your pet is likely to be specially attuned to your problems and we can provide the training to bring out their potential as a dedicated companion both at home and in the public space.

Online course

This course is available via the Internet to people who want to train from the comfort of their home and neighborhood.

In-Person course

This course is available in the UK, Ireland and parts of the US.

The course begins by introducing the training principles, the necessary theoretical knowledge and building an understating of what to expect from your psychiatric service dog.

We will help you know more about how your dog communicates and how they can respond to your emotional state and behaviour cues.

The practical training will cover Eye Contact, Obedience Skills, Opening and Closing Tasks, Standing Up and Pushing a Cart, Deep Pressure Therapy.

We look forward to hearing from people with loving and healthy dogs who may consider training their own psychiatric service dog. Please, use the Contact Us page.
